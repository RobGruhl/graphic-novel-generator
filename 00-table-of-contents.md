# Silk Road: Table of Contents

A Lovecraftian dark fantasy set in 900 AD along the Silk Road, following four cursed protagonists who become entangled with cosmic horrors, werewolf cults, and ancient evils.

---

## Prologues (Character Origins)

### [Prologue I: The Shepherd Who Became a Wolf](input/narrative/prologue-01-giovanni.md)
**Characters:** Giovanni, Marco (merchant), Bishop Paulus, Renigius
**Locations:** Venice, Ravenna, Aquileia, Constantinople

Father Giovanni's journey from Venetian farm boy to merchant to priest to Church assassin. Under Bishop Paulus, he becomes a killer for the Church, eliminating heretics with cold efficiency. After being sent to assassinate the Patriarch of Aquileia, he's betrayed and flees east along the Silk Road, hunted by the Church's agents.

### [Prologue II: The Daughter of Shadows](input/narrative/prologue-02-bansi.md)
**Characters:** Bansi (Iksha Bhima), Raja Kanishka (father), Vikrahma (brother), Maya (mother)
**Locations:** Srinagar, Palace of Sonapura, Kashmir

Fifth daughter of Raja Kanishka, Iksha escapes her fate as a political bride by disguising herself as servants and attending her brothers' lessons. At thirteen, learning she'll be sold to a fifty-three-year-old Shahi, she flees. She joins the Bahn-Sa-Sahn thieves' guild, trains for two years, and becomes a devotee of Kali. Now at eighteen, she is Bansi—thief, fighter, and devotee of the Dark Mother.

### [Prologue III: The Consort of Shub-Niggurath](input/narrative/prologue-03-eskil.md)
**Characters:** Eskil, Leif (brother), Anna, Sofie, Saga, Kari, Alexandra
**Locations:** Eysýsla (Saaremaa island), Underryd bog, Undercroft, Kyiv arena

On the Baltic island of Saaremaa, a cult of young women serves Shub-Niggurath through ritual sacrifice. Eskil is chosen as "Meat" for their ritual, but his brother Leif's rune magic swaps their places at the moment of death. The ritual fails; the Messenger rages; Eysýsla is destroyed by purple fire. Eskil escapes, cursed with supernaturally delicious blood that makes him prey to cosmic horrors forever. He forges a permanent helm and collar to prevent himself from consuming his own blood, becomes a gladiator in Kyiv, then a guild executioner hunting monsters.

### [Prologue IV: The Raven of the Emperor](input/narrative/prologue-04-lueduo.md)
**Characters:** Lüèduó (Raven), Wei (majordomo), Lady Mei, Minister Zhang, the Nagini
**Locations:** Taklamakan desert, Tang court (Chang'an), Serpent's Rest

A Uyghur dancer with something ancient in his blood, Lüèduó is taken as a hostage to the Tang court where his beauty and skill make him a court favorite. He becomes a spy for the eunuch majordomo Wei, seducing nobles and gathering secrets. When caught with Minister Zhang's wife, he's exiled to investigate disappearances in the west. In the ruins called Serpent's Rest, he encounters the Nagini—an ancient serpent entity who claims his bloodline and marks him with her power. He flees west, meets Eskil, and they travel together toward Samarkand.

## Episode I: The Golden Road

### [Chapter 1: The Golden Road to Samarkand](input/narrative/episode-01-ch01-golden-road.md)
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Hind, Sadia, Rahzalahn, Abu-Rayhan, Nizack, Theotomere
**Locations:** Samarkand market, Hind's caravan camp

The protagonists meet by chance in Samarkand when Theotomere (a Church assassin) attacks Giovanni. Bansi, Eskil, and Gan intervene. Sadia leads them to Hind's caravan. They're hired as guards and bond over shared stories. Giovanni catches Bansi stealing and offers mercy. Rahzalahn confronts Eskil about Norse raiders destroying her village; they reach an uneasy truce. Hind offers them a job: retrieve an amulet from a cursed castle with the guide Nizack.

### [Chapter 2: The Iron Gates](input/narrative/episode-01-ch02-iron-gates.md)
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Dog, Sadia, Abu-Rayhan, Nizack
**Locations:** Road to Iron Gates, abandoned village, cursed castle exterior

Three days riding toward the Iron Gates. Dog senses Lüèduó's serpent nature and attacks; Lüèduó subdues him telepathically. They share stories around the fire. Bandits attack at night—Eskil cleaves their leader in half, revealing his berserker nature. At the village, they find corpses in the well. Nizack betrays them and vanishes. Ghouls rise. They fight to the castle, Eskil marking the ghouls with witch-fire, Giovanni's prayers destroying them from the battlements.

### [Chapter 3: The Amulet](input/narrative/episode-01-ch03-amulet.md)
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Dog, Sadia, Abu-Rayhan, Nizack, Bird-thing
**Locations:** Cursed castle interior, underground temple

They enter the castle, descend into alien architecture. Bronze doors that breathe, living vines, watching statues. At an altar beneath a cyclopean figure, Lüèduó reaches for the amulet. A horrific bird-thing emerges. Paralyzed by terror, Lüèduó calls on the Nagini—serpentine coils restrain the horror. Eskil fights Nizack, who dissolves into shadow. They escape with the amulet. The caravan heads west toward Merv. Lüèduó now owes the Nagini a debt.

## Episode II: The Moon Over Merv

### Chapter 4: The Oasis of Merv
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Rahzalahn, Abu-Rayhan, Sadia, Hind, Earmack, the Nagini
**Locations:** Merv oasis, Abu-Rayhan's house, storytelling circle

The caravan reaches Merv. Lüèduó tells tales at the storytelling circle. The Nagini appears in a vision, warning of danger from the amulet. A girl named Earmack arrives on horseback, bloodied and terrified. She escaped a werewolf cult led by Karishkir (a 300-year-old werewolf queen) with her lovers: Nizack (alive!), Drekhlo, and "The Khan of the Nine Skulls." They had been sacrificing captives at a kurgan burial mound.

### Chapter 5: The Howling
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Sadia, Earmack, werewolves
**Locations:** Merv streets, Abu-Rayhan's house, steppe

Lüèduó and Bansi find Sadia drunk in a wine shop. Giovanni and Eskil encounter a werewolf in the streets—they kill it, bring the body to Abu-Rayhan. Silver through the heart and prayer are the only way to ensure death. They learn Karishkir has been alive for centuries. The company rides out to stop the cult. Psychic assault: Karishkir's voice calls to Earmack, visions of bloody fangs assault Eskil and Bansi. They ready for battle.

## Episode III: To the Kurgan

### Chapter 6: A Wound in the Earth
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Sadia, Earmack, Isaac (Order of Hermes)
**Locations:** Kurgan burial mound, Abu-Rayhan's house (via portal), catacombs

They find the kurgan—flayed skins on poles, the cult sacrificed their own. A portal opens to Abu-Rayhan's ransacked house. Karishkir used the psychic attack to steal the amulet. Rahzalahn killed one attacker with her hairpin. They chase through the portal into ancient catacombs. Earmack is killed by a werewolf. Giovanni gives her last rites. They fight deeper into the tunnels.

### Chapter 7: The Chapel of Bones
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Sadia, Rahzalahn, Isaac, Karishkir, Drekhlo, Nizack
**Locations:** Chapel of Bones (underground cathedral), Merv

The Chapel of Bones: a cathedral of death with walls of skulls. Karishkir stands behind an altar where Rahzalahn is bound for sacrifice. Nizack holds the ritual dagger. Drekhlo guards. Battle erupts. Bansi frees Rahzalahn. Eskil duels Drekhlo. Lüèduó distracts Karishkir but she seizes him. Isaac's blinding light spell forces Karishkir and Drekhlo to flee through a portal. Nizack is killed—Rahzalahn's hairpin in his throat, then silver daggers through his heart. He disintegrates. The amulet is recovered. The Ahmira of Merv takes half their treasure but lets them go.

## Episode IV: Shiraz and the Sea

### Chapter 8: The Road to Shiraz
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Sadia, Rahzalahn, Hind, various bandits
**Locations:** Mountain passes, caravan route to Shiraz

The caravan departs Merv. Bandits attack twice in the passes—easily defeated. The company bonds into an effective fighting unit. Eskil sleeps alone; at night, insects drawn by his cursed blood feed themselves to his sleeping form.

### Chapter 9: The Sea of Oman
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Dog, Sadia, Hind, Rahzalahn, Taibiri, ship crew
**Locations:** Shiraz, Sea of Oman, pirate ship

They reach Shiraz and take ship. Taibiri of Shendi joins as their guide. Pirates attack; the party boards the enemy vessel. Gan climbs the mast. Bansi duels the pirate captain. Eskil wades through enemies. The pirates are defeated; they take the ship as prize.

### Chapter 10: The Burning Sands
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Sadia, Abu-Rayhan, Hind
**Locations:** Red Sea port of Aidhab, Egyptian desert

They land at Aidhab. Abu-Rayhan believes the amulet's origins lie in Stygian temples beneath the Egyptian sands. They purchase camels and supplies. Into the desert they ride, toward the Valley of the Dead.

## Episode V: Under the Accursed Sands

### Chapter 11: Landfall into Burning Sands
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Sadia, Abu-Rayhan, desert raiders
**Locations:** Egyptian desert, ancient tombs

Desert travel. They encounter raiders and defeat them. Ancient tombs loom ahead. Strange symbols on the stones match those on the amulet.

### Chapter 12: The Valley of the Dead
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Sadia, Akivasha
**Locations:** Valley of the Dead, Stygian tomb

In the Valley of the Dead, they find an entrance to the Stygian tomb. Within: Akivasha, an ancient mummified sorceress who has waited millennia. She recognizes the amulet and speaks of its creation. The tomb holds answers about the cosmic forces they've been battling.

### Chapter 13: Temple Beneath Time
**Characters:** Giovanni, Bansi, Eskil, Lüèduó, Gan, Sadia, various entities
**Locations:** Temple beneath the sands

The deepest chambers reveal the Temple Beneath Time. Ancient beings, cosmic geometries, revelations about the Khan of the Nine Skulls and Karishkir's ultimate plans. The amulet's true purpose is revealed. They must decide: destroy it, use it, or flee.

## Key Characters

### Protagonists
- **Fr. Giovanni** - Venetian priest/assassin, carries crucifix and blessed blade
- **Bansi (Iksha Bhima)** - Kashmiri thief, devotee of Kali, member of Bahn-Sa-Sahn guild
- **Eskil** - Norse executioner, 6'8", permanent steel helm/collar, cursed blood
- **Lüèduó (Raven)** - Uyghur dancer/spy, serpent blood, amber eyes, compulsive gambler

### Allies
- **Hind** - Qatari merchant, caravan leader, commands authority
- **Sadia** - Warrior woman, bracers on forearms, drinks heavily
- **Rahzalahn** - Oud player, Persian heritage, silver hairpins as weapons
- **Abu-Rayhan** - Scholar, cartographer, studies the amulet
- **Gan** - Steppe warrior with Dog and falcon companions
- **Taibiri** - Guide for sea voyage
- **Isaac** - Order of Hermes mage, casts blinding light

### Antagonists
- **Karishkir** - Werewolf queen, 300+ years old, beautiful and terrible voice
- **Drekhlo (Dragon-claw)** - Werewolf warrior, great sword, Karishkir's lover
- **Nizack** - Sorcerer, Karishkir's lover, killed at Chapel of Bones
- **Theotomere** - Church assassin hunting Giovanni
- **The Khan of the Nine Skulls** - Central antagonist (not yet encountered)

### Cosmic Entities
- **The Nagini** - Ancient serpent being, claims Lüèduó's bloodline
- **Shub-Niggurath (The Goat)** - Mother of a Thousand Young, hunts Eskil
- **The Messenger** - Spawn of the Goat, destroyed Eysýsla

---

## Key Locations

- **Samarkand** - Great trade hub, silk markets
- **Merv** - Oasis city, catacombs beneath
- **Shiraz** - Persian city, departure for sea voyage
- **Eysýsla** - Burned Baltic island (Eskil's origin, destroyed)
- **Underryd Bog** - Ritual site of Shub-Niggurath cult
- **Iron Gates** - Boundary between settled lands and steppe
- **Cursed Castle** - Contains alien architecture, cyclopean figure, bird-thing
- **Kurgan** - Steppe burial mound, werewolf cult base
- **Chapel of Bones** - Underground cathedral, walls of skulls
- **Valley of the Dead** - Egyptian site with Stygian tombs
